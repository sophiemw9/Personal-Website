Contrast

Contrast within design is important to attract the reader to the design. The idea behind contrast is to avoid similar elements on the page, if they are not the same you must make them drastically different. To display this principle, I split the page in half following the design of the logo, although I flipped the white and red to be on opposite sides of the line to differ from the logo. By splitting the background and elements, I was able to easily display contrast on the page.

Alignment

The principle of alignment states that everything placed has a purpose and some visual connection with another element on the page. Within my design, the information about the event along with the website have been placed aligned along the left edge of the poster, whereas the date, location and title of the event have been placed aligned along the centre of the page. I have chosen to use two separate points of alignment to help navigate the readers eyes to the most vital information.

Repetition

Repetition is the repeated use of elements including shapes, colours, fonts, textures, graphic content, textures, etc. This principle helps strengthen the unity throughout the page. For my design, I chose to repeat the polygon shape to make the background appear similar to a beehive. The placement of each letter within each shape, gives purpose to the polygons and helps connect the background to the foreground.

Proximity

The principle of proximity states that all related content should be close to each other to improve readability. These elements then become one visual unit rather than multiple separate ones. For my design, I kept the proximity of the general information close together. Since each part of information still belongs on its own line, the readability is still good. To separate the different elements, I added the film strips with old cinema photos.
