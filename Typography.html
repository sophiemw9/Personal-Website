Axial

An axial design is following an imaginary line, the design can either be symmetrical or asymmetrical. The line does not need to be straight. I chose to go for a somewhat symmetrical design where the information is all based on the centre of my axis. I placed mine on a tilt because I wanted to keep it simple. I changed the font of the most important information to extra bold, to make it more prominent at a first glance.

Dilational

A dilational design is where all elements expand from a central point in a circular fashion. I have utilised this design technique by creating a vinyl like design. The title of the exhibition is large and in bold to draw the attention to the details.

Radial

A radial design is based off a single focal point and all other elements are extended from this one point. I decided to create my focal point on the edge of the page due to the amount of information to be shared. The circle being a gradient made sense to me as it meant that none of the text was overpowered by the bold colour.
